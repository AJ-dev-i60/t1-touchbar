03 · the designer · craft altitude
Layer Loom — stack blocks into something striking
Open any part and it blooms large. Its look is a stack of layers — background, texture, material, icon, effects, motion — that you drag to reorder. Any layer can carry a timing envelope, so a press can flare and fade back on its own clock.

04 · two surfaces, one relationship
Background & materials
The background is its own sibling surface — never a parent of the parts. What ties them together is one choice: a part's material. That single decision is how “alive background + quiet glass” and “black background + loud buttons” both fall out — plentiful options, never a wall of knobs.

05 · the philosophy
A finite kit — combinable into the spectacular
Every block is one the system understands. Layer a handful and the result looks bespoke enough to make people ask how the hell did you do that — without the app ever drowning anyone in detail.

06 · for the hardcore · at the edge
Extend by spec · share whole scenes
Power users author icons, textures and effects in their own tools, conform to a spec, and import them — where each becomes just another stackable, timeable layer. Whole scenes package into a single downloadable file the community can share and remix. The import seam sits at the edge, never between you and the live hardware.
